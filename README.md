# IMDb Movie Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project investigates the factors that influence the success of a movie on IMDB. For this study, "success" is defined by high IMDB ratings. The insights generated are designed to help movie producers, directors, and investors make informed, data-driven decisions for future cinematic projects.

## 📊 Project Objectives

* Analyze the distribution of movie genres and their specific impact on IMDb scores.


* Evaluate the relationship between movie duration and audience ratings.


* Examine the linguistic distribution of movies within the dataset.


* Assess the influence of individual directors on movie ratings.


* Explore the correlation between movie budgets and their overall financial success.



## 🛠️ Tech Stack

* 
**MS Excel**: Used as the primary tool for data exploration, cleaning, complex analysis, and visualization.


* 
**MS PowerPoint**: Used to compile and present the final business report and insights.



## 🗂️ Data Preprocessing & Cleaning

The raw dataset originally contained **28 columns and 5,044 rows**. To prepare the data for meaningful analysis, several preprocessing steps were taken:

* 
**Data Reduction**: Removed unnecessary columns and rows with critical missing values, resulting in a cleaned dataset of **16 columns and 4,870 rows**.


* 
**Feature Engineering**: Created a new **"Profit"** column to better track financial performance.


* 
**General Cleaning**: Handled duplicate entries, converted data types for consistency, and managed missing values in financial fields by focusing on average metrics.



## 📈 Key Insights & Findings

* 
**High-Performing Genres**: Movies in the **Biography, Documentary, Film-Noir, and Music** genres consistently achieve average IMDB scores greater than **7.0**.


* 
**Duration Impact**: Analysis indicates that the duration of a movie does not have a significant impact on its IMDb score.


* 
**Language Distribution**: The vast majority of movies in the analyzed dataset are in the **English** language.


* 
**Director Influence**: Director **Sadyk Sher-Niyak** was found to have the highest average IMDb score among all directors in the study.


* 
**Financial Peak**: The movie **Avatar** recorded the highest total profit within the dataset.
